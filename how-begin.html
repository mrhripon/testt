How everything begin

While I was working in Houston, Texas, I went on several business conferences. I met scores of people and expanded my network at each conference. One small problem was I did not have any business cards. My supervisor was always extremely busy and brushed off providing company-certified business cards. So, there I was, collecting business cards from everyone and everybody. Traditionally, after every conference, I send follow-up emails to all encounters, describing highlights in the encounter and sharing contact information.

After the last conference, I gathered the business cards, preparing to deliver messages. While scrummaging through the stacks of recently obtained business cards, it became apparent I was not going to remember most of the new connections. I could recall at least a dozen conversations and most business cards have no face to attach to the name. I pondered the solution and an idea struck: Why aren‚Äôt business cards digital? Solution

I decided to create Transfyr, the business card app for creating, storing, and sharing business cards. Now sharing contact information is as quick and easy as a QR image scan. Users can write notes about contacts and group contacts together. More features are coming soon.

The Transfyr team hopes you enjoy using this eco-friendly app! Each year, 100 billion business cards are produced worldwide leading to the potential loss of approximately 6 million trees. Our funding comes from donations and advertisements. Please donate and share today
